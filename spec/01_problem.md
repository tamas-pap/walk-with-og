# Problem

Modern people carry ancient bodies through abstract pressure.

Emails, phones, deadlines, comparison, perfectionism, and unfinished work are not predators, fires, or exile. But the body often answers as if they are: tight shoulders, noisy head, shallow breath, restless scrolling, avoidance, collapse on the couch.

They know a walk would help. The problem is not knowledge. The problem is that, at the exact moment walking would work, modern pressure has made the simple thing feel too flat, too optional, or too lonely to start.

## Why existing options miss

- **Fitness apps add obligation.** Steps, streaks, zones, and progress metrics turn the walk into one more thing to perform.
- **Gamified apps add pull without depth.** Points, quests, and collectibles may get someone outside, but the walk can feel disconnected from the real pressure they are carrying.
- **Wellness and therapy framing can feel too heavy.** The user may need relief and company, not a session, diagnosis, or self-improvement project.

## Why walking

Walking is the smallest useful intervention that involves the whole person: body, attention, environment, and time.

It does not require gear, performance, expertise, or belief. It gives the body a way to downshift, lets attention leave the screen, and creates enough movement for stuck thoughts to loosen.

## Why Og

Og gives the walk a reason to begin and a lens that makes it matter.

He understands the modern problem, but reads it through ancient human needs: safety, shelter, food, rest, tribe, movement, attention, courage. That makes modern stress feel less sacred and more movable.

Og is not a coach telling the user to optimize. He is grounded company beside them, stripping the noise down until the next small human move is visible.

## Product promise

A short walk with Og turns modern overload into something simpler, bodily, and walkable.

Not fitness. Not therapy. Not productivity. A companion who helps the user leave the room, move through the pressure, and come back clearer than they left.
